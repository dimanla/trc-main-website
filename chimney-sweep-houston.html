Choosing the Right Inspection Level
Level 1 Inspection

Routine Annual Check

The standard inspection for chimneys with unchanged systems. We verify the visible portions of the interior and exterior to ensure basic structural soundness and freedom from obstructions.

  • Visual check of flue
  • Basic soundness verification
Level 2 Inspection

Real Estate / Minor Changes

Required when buying/selling a home, adding a new liner, or changing fuel types. Includes a deeper video scan of internal surfaces to find hidden cracks or damage.

  • Video Scanning
  • Attic/Crawlspace check
Level 3 Inspection

Major Hazard / Structural

A specialized, invasive inspection deemed necessary when serious hazards are suspected. May involve removing components (like a crown or wall) to access hidden areas.

  • Invasive access
  • Confirming suspected hazards

Chimney Sweep FAQs
How often should I have my chimney swept in Houston?

The CSIA recommends an annual inspection, regardless of how often you use your fireplace. If you burn one cord of wood (4x4x8 ft stack), you should definitely schedule a cleaning to remove dangerous creosote buildup.

Why is creosote dangerous for my home?

Creosote is a highly flammable byproduct of burning wood. If allowed to accumulate in your flue, it can easily ignite and cause a chimney fire that can spread to your attic and roof.

What is the difference between a Level 1 and Level 2 inspection?

A Level 1 inspection is for routine annual cleaning where nothing has changed. A Level 2 inspection, which includes video scanning, is required when selling a home, after a storm event, or when changing fuel types (e.g., from wood to gas).

Can you remove birds or animals from my chimney?

Yes. Houston chimneys often attract swifts, raccoons, and squirrels. We safely remove the animals (respecting protected species) and can install a heavy-duty cap to prevent future entry.

Why does my fireplace smell bad in the summer?

Humid Houston summers create a "reverse draft" that pulls soot odors down into your cool home. A professional cleaning removes the odor source, and a top-sealing damper can help seal out the humidity.

Do gas fireplaces need to be cleaned?

Yes. While they don't produce creosote, gas logs can deposit carbon soot, and the flue can still get blocked by bird nests or debris, causing deadly Carbon Monoxide to back up into your home.

What happens if I have a chimney fire?

Chimney fires can damage the clay flue liners, cracking them and allowing heat to reach combustible wood framing. If you suspect a fire occurred, do not use the fireplace until a certified technician inspects it.

Do I really need a chimney cap?

Absolutely. A cap acts as an umbrella, preventing rain from rotting your smoke shelf and damper. It also has a mesh screen to keep out birds, squirrels, and roof rats common in our area.

How do I know if my chimney is leaking?

Signs include water stains on your ceiling near the fireplace, a rusted damper, or white powdery stains (efflorescence) on the exterior brickwork. We can perform a water test to find the leak source.

What is "flue waterproofing" and why do I need it?

Bricks are like sponges. In our wet climate, water absorption causes "spalling" (face popping off). We apply a breathable waterproofing agent that stops water entry but allows the brick to breathe.

My fireplace smokes up the room. Can you fix it?

Smoke issues are often caused by usage errors (cold flue), sizing issues, or draft problems. We perform a "draft test" to diagnose why the smoke isn't drawing upward and propose a solution.

How long does a chimney sweeping appointment take?

A standard cleaning and inspection typically takes 45 minutes to an hour. We use drop cloths and HEPA vacuums to ensure absolutely zero mess enters your home.

Are your technicians certified?

Yes, Texas Right Choice technicians follow strict industry standards for safety and inspection protocols, ensuring your fireplace is safe for your family to enjoy.

Can you repair the mortar on top of my chimney?

Yes, that is called the "chimney crown." If it cracks, water pours straight into the chimney walls. We can repair hairline cracks or install a brand new concrete crown with a proper drip edge.

What area of Houston do you serve?

We serve the entire Greater Houston metroplex, including Katy, Sugar Land, The Woodlands, Pearland, Cypress, and everywhere in between.
